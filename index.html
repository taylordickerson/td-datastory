ENGL 3804 Data Project

Taylor Dickerson

This is the Data Story from Spring 2018, tracking how we use media in our day-to-day use.


Throughout the semester, our class has studied the way we use data. Data is most often thought to be "big data", but this class has helped me see that anything can be collected and studied to provide insight into a larger whole. Our projects were modeled after the "Dear Data" project, and through Giorgia Lupi and Stefanie Posavec's correspondence, I learned that not all data collection had to be so rigid and mechanical. Through their visual designs, I came to appreciate more of the human side of data.

Data Collection


For the data collection project, I tracked my mood with what emojis I sent in my messages. I tracked my messages over my phone's messaging app, Twitter, Line (a messaging app), and email. This project made me more aware of my emoji use and how I use them to illustrate certain moods. One drawback in the assignment was that I was aware of my behavior which may have influenced my use of emojis over the week, but I know this wasn't exactly a detractor to this particular assignment.

Data Visualization


We then used the results from our Data Collection to create our Data Visualization. For this assignment, we had to pick a narrative that we wanted to tell through our Visualization. For mine, I chose to represent my data through a chord diagram. Moods are abstract, so I centered them in my graph and with different weighted lines, I connected the emojis to those moods.

Explained Video


For my Explained Video, I decided to focus on the development of emojis. Since my previous projects centered around emojis, I wanted to explore exactly who is in charge of the process and some of the controversies that have arisen because of emojis. I made the video using iMovie with clips and pictures from Pixabay and from screen-grabs that I made myself. Researching emojis gave me greater understanding how companies create them.
